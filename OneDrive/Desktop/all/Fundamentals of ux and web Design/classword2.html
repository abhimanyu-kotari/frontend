NAMMA BENGALURU

Bangalore Palace

Bengaluru Palace is in the midst of the hustle and bustle so-called electronic city of Bengaluru, Karnataka. The palace is situated between Sadashivanagar and Jayamahal. Surrounded by beautiful gardens, the palace attracts tourists in large numbers. Also, this is preferred destination for important events and cultural programs. The wooden structure of the palace, with splendid carvings both inside and outside depicting different architectural styles, is reminiscent of the royal culture it represents. Many cultural events and social functions, such as marriage ceremony, exhibitions, musical concerts, sports events, etc., are held in the palace grounds for the past few years. There is an amusement park, better known as Fun World, inside the palace complex. An audio tour of one-hour duration is available in seven languages: English, French, German, Italian, Spanish, Hindi and Kannada.  

TIMINGS: All days of the week from 10 am to 5 pm.


ISKON Temple Bangalore

The ISKCON Sri Radha Krishna Temple in Bengaluru, India is a large cultural complex that promotes spiritual learning and Vedic culture: Location The temple is located in Rajajinagar, Bengaluru. Features The temple is built on a hillock and features a blend of modern and traditional architecture. It also has a 17-meter gold-plated flag and 8.5-meter Kalash Shikaras. Deities The temple houses several deities, including Krishna, Balaram, Radha, Chaitanya Mahaprabhu, Nithyananda Prabhu, Prahlada Narasimha, Srila Prabhupada, and Srinivasa Govinda. Activities The temple offers spiritual and educational programs for the public and devotees. It also hosts an annual chariot procession that draws many devotees. History The temple was inaugurated in 1997 by former President of India, Sri Shankar Dayal Sharma. How to get there The temple is about 5–6 kilometers from the central bus station and railway station. You can also take the Metro Rail to Mahalakshmi station and walk five minutes to the temple. ISKCON (International Society for Krishna Consciousness) is a religious organization with millions of devotees worldwide. It was founded in New York City in 1966 by AC Bhaktivedanta Swami to promote the practice of Krishna Consciousness.
